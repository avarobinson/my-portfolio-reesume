About

My research internship with Snap Inc. was focused on studying and building co-located experiences using AR. With previous research guiding our proccess, my team and I ideated, prototyped, iterated, and studied multiple co-located experiences. We built 5 Snapchat Lenses to support in-person experiences using Lens Studio and proceded with a study of each lens. Our findings are described in a paper we are submitting to CHI (in progress).

Below I highlight the two lenses I developed: Feeture Films and Milky Way.


Feeture Films

Feeture Films is a Snapchat Lens that invovles two users telling a story with their feet. Building off of the existing behavior of reading a bedtime story, users share one device and sit next to each other, normally on a bed or couch. Using Snap's Machine Learning feet detection, users' feet are augmented into jungle themed sock puppets. Players tell a story with their feet by navigating through scenes with text queues. We found a common pattern in children's books of guided discovery, as you can see in find Spot below. To provide structure, yet allow for creative storytelling we followed a similar pattern of guided discovery by allowing users to hover with their feet over different objects like a rock to discover other animals.
